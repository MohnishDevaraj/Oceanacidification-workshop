About Center for Earth and Atmospheric Sciences

Centre for Earth and Atmospheric Sciences (CEAS) established at Sathyabama Institute of Science and Technology in accordance with the goal of establishing Centre of excellence in frontier areas of Earth and Atmospheric Sciences in the year 2010, a joint initiative of Indira Gandhi Centre for Atomic Research (IGCAR), Kalpakkam. The Centre has well equipped laboratories to give a wide exposure to the students for practical knowledge and hands on experience. The Centre, in addition to undertaking research and development projects assist Sathyabama Institute of Science and Technology academically in running the PG and Doctoral programmes in the areas of Earth and Atmospheric Sciences. Further, short term training programmes are also offered on various themes of topical interest. The Centre has a multidisciplinary team of highly qualified Scientists in the area of Environmental Geochemistry, Earth and Environmental Science, Climate Change, Remote Sensing and GIS, Coastal Zone management.

About the Workshop

The acidity of the world’s oceans is increasing by absorbing rising atmospheric CO2. Ocean acidification can lead to global marine biodiversity loss. This workshop will provide a platform for young scientists from India to get acquainted with the latest scientific developments in the field of ocean acidification research through interaction with some of the leading scientists recognized globally and nationally. One of the important outcomes of this workshop will be to facilitate collaborative research on Ocean acidification in South Asia.

The workshop will cover the following topics:

  • Marine chemistry and Ocean Acidification

  • Ocean acidification research and Data analysis

  • Future research needs in ocean acidification

  • Impacts of Ocean acidification on Marine ecosystem

  • Scaling up to humans: the socioeconomics of ocean acidification
